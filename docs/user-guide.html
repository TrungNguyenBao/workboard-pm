A-ERP User Guide

Complete guide to using the Agentic Enterprise Resource Platform — covering Projects, Warehouse, HR, and Sales management.

Version 1.0  ·  Last updated March 2026  ·  For all roles: Admin, Member, Guest

1. Welcome to A-ERP

A-ERP is an Agentic Enterprise Resource Platform that brings all your core business operations into one connected workspace. It is built around four modules — each covering a distinct business domain — that share a common authentication, notification, and search layer.

PMS Project Management
Tasks, boards, timelines, goals, and custom fields for teams of any size.
WMS Warehouse Management
Products, inventory, warehouses, devices, and supplier records.
HRM Human Resources
Employee lifecycle from recruitment through offboarding, plus payroll and performance.
CRM Sales Management
Contact database and deal pipeline from Lead to Closed Won.

All four modules share the same workspace, user accounts, role-based access control, real-time notifications, and global search. You switch between modules using the module switcher in the left sidebar — your context (workspace, permissions) carries across automatically.

Creating an Account

Navigate to /register to create a new account. Fill in the registration form:

  1. Full Name — displayed in the app, visible to teammates.
  2. Email address — used to log in; must be unique across the platform.
  3. Password — minimum 8 characters. Use the eye icon on the right side of the password field to toggle visibility while typing.

After submitting, you will be redirected to the login page to sign in with your new credentials.

✓
Tip Use a strong, unique password. A-ERP uses secure bcrypt hashing and stores refresh tokens as HttpOnly cookies — your credentials are never exposed to client-side scripts.

Logging In

Navigate to /login and enter your email address and password. After a successful login, the platform redirects you to My Tasks — your personal task inbox across all projects.

A-ERP uses a dual-token authentication scheme:

  • Access token — short-lived JWT held in memory (not localStorage). Sent with every API request via the Authorization: Bearer header.
  • Refresh token — longer-lived token stored in an HttpOnly cookie (30-day expiry). Used to obtain a new access token automatically when the current one expires, so sessions persist across page refreshes without requiring you to log in again.
ⓘ
Session Persistence Closing the browser tab does not log you out. Your session is maintained via the HttpOnly refresh cookie until it expires after 30 days of inactivity, or until you explicitly log out.

First-Time Setup

The first time you log in, a Workspace Creation dialog appears automatically. Every user must belong to a workspace to access any module.

  1. Enter a Workspace Name (e.g. "Acme Corp" or "Product Team"). A URL-friendly slug is generated automatically — for example, "Acme Corp" becomes acme-corp.
  2. Click Create Workspace. You are automatically assigned the Admin role in the new workspace.
  3. You can now invite team members from the Members page /members.

If someone invites you to an existing workspace, you will join that workspace directly and skip this step. You can also create additional workspaces later from the workspace picker in the sidebar header.

Navigation

Sidebar

The left sidebar (240px wide) is the primary navigation surface. It contains:

  • Workspace picker — at the top. Click to switch between workspaces you belong to, or create a new one.
  • Module switcher — toggle between PMS, WMS, HRM, and CRM. The sidebar navigation links below change to reflect the active module.
  • Module navigation links — section-specific pages for the active module (e.g. Dashboard, My Tasks, Projects for PMS).
  • User footer — at the bottom. Shows your avatar and name, with a link to Settings and a Logout button.

On screens narrower than 768px, the sidebar collapses and is accessible via the hamburger menu button in the top bar. Tap anywhere outside the sidebar to close it.

Top Bar

The 48px-high top bar appears on every page and provides:

  • Breadcrumb — shows your current location, e.g. "Projects / Sprint Q2 / Board".
  • Search — click the search icon or press Ctrl+K to open the command palette for full-text search across tasks and projects.
  • Dark mode toggle — switches the app-wide theme. Preference is saved to localStorage so it persists across sessions.
  • Notifications bell — shows unread notification count (displays "9+" when more than 9 unread). Click to open the notification dropdown.

Language

A-ERP supports English and Vietnamese. To switch language:

  • Click your user avatar in the sidebar footer and select Settings.
  • Under the Preferences section, choose your preferred language from the dropdown.

Language preference is saved to localStorage and persists across sessions without requiring an account-level change. You can also switch quickly from the language selector in the sidebar footer (displayed as a flag icon or "EN / VI" label).

2. Workspace Management

A workspace is the top-level container for all your data — projects, employees, inventory, contacts, and deals are all scoped to a single workspace. You can belong to multiple workspaces with different roles in each.

Switching Workspaces

Click the workspace name at the top of the sidebar to open a dropdown listing all workspaces you belong to. Click any workspace to switch immediately. The active module and page are preserved if the same route exists in the new workspace.

Renaming a Workspace

Admin In the workspace dropdown, click the pencil icon next to the current workspace name to enter inline-edit mode. Type the new name and press Enter to save. The URL slug does not change when you rename a workspace.

Creating a New Workspace

At the bottom of the workspace picker dropdown, click + New Workspace. The same creation dialog as first-time setup appears. You will be assigned as Admin of the new workspace.

Members Page Admin

Navigate to /members to manage everyone in your workspace. The Members page shows a table of all current members with:

  • Avatar — initials or uploaded image.
  • Name & Email — user identity.
  • Role badge — Admin, Member, or Guest.
  • Actions — change role (dropdown) or remove from workspace (trash icon).

Inviting Members

Click Invite Member (top right). Enter the invitee's email address, select a role (Admin / Member / Guest), and click Send Invite. An invitation email is sent. Once the recipient accepts, they appear in the members list.

Changing Roles

Click the role badge or dropdown next to any member to change their workspace role. Role changes take effect immediately — the user's next request will use the updated permissions.

⚠
Caution Removing a member revokes access immediately. Their data (tasks, comments, etc.) is retained but will be unassigned or shown with a deleted-user indicator.

PMS 3. Dashboard

The PMS Dashboard at /pms/dashboard gives a workspace-wide snapshot of all project activity. Four KPI cards show the key numbers at a glance:

  • Total Tasks — all open tasks across every project you have access to.
  • Overdue — tasks with a due date in the past that are not yet completed. Displayed in red to draw attention.
  • Completed This Week — tasks marked done in the last 7 days.
  • Active Projects — count of non-archived projects in the workspace.

Below the KPI row, a Tasks by Priority bar chart shows the distribution of open tasks across High, Medium, Low, and None priority levels — useful for spotting if your team is overloaded with high-priority work.

My Tasks

/pms/my-tasks is the default landing page after login. It consolidates every task assigned to you across all projects into one view, grouped into time-based buckets:

  • Overdue — past due date and not yet complete. Shown in red.
  • Today — due today. Shown in amber.
  • Upcoming — due within the next 7 days.
  • Later — due beyond 7 days, or no due date set.

Click any task row to open the full Task Details drawer without leaving My Tasks. You can complete tasks, update due dates, reassign, and add comments directly from this view.

✓
Daily Habit Start each day on My Tasks. The Overdue and Today buckets give you an immediate picture of what needs attention before you dive into project-level work.

Creating a Project

  1. In the PMS sidebar, click the + button next to the "Projects" section header.
  2. Enter a project name. This appears in the sidebar and on all task cards.
  3. Select a color from the 12-color swatch palette. The color appears as a dot in the sidebar and as the kanban card left-border accent.
  4. Optionally add a description to explain the project's purpose.
  5. Click Create. You are taken directly to the new project's Board view.

You are automatically assigned as the project Owner. You can invite additional members from Project Settings.

Board View

/pms/projects/:id/board — The Kanban board is the most visual way to manage a project. Each section becomes a column. Tasks are draggable cards within and between columns.

Working with Columns

  • Click + Add Section (rightmost column) to create a new column.
  • Click a column header to rename the section inline.
  • Drag columns left or right to reorder them.

Task Cards

Each card shows the task title, priority dot (color-coded), due date, assignee avatar, and attachment count. Drag the grip handle on the left side of a card to move it within or between columns. The position is saved using fractional indexing — inserting a task between two existing tasks assigns it a position exactly halfway between their positions, preventing conflicts during concurrent moves.

Filters

Use the filter bar above the board to narrow cards by Priority (High / Medium / Low / None) or Status. Multiple filters can be combined. Filtered columns show only matching cards; empty filtered columns are still displayed to preserve layout.

Adding Tasks

Click + Add Task at the bottom of any column, or click the + card button that appears on hover. Enter the title and press Enter to create instantly.

List View

/pms/projects/:id/list — The List view presents tasks in a compact table grouped by section. Each section is collapsible (click the chevron next to the section name).

Columns

The table shows: Checkbox (complete toggle), Title, Priority (colored dot + label), Due Date, and Assignee (avatar). Column widths are fixed to maximize information density.

Inline Task Creation

Click + Add Task at the bottom of any section group to append a new task inline. Type the title and press Enter to save, or Esc to cancel.

Search, Filter & Sort

  • Inline search — the search box above the table filters tasks by title in real time (client-side).
  • Filter — filter by Priority, Assignee, or Due Date range.
  • Sort — sort by Due Date, Priority, or Assignee. Click a column header to toggle ascending/descending.

Calendar View

/pms/projects/:id/calendar — The Calendar view displays tasks on a monthly grid based on their due dates. It is ideal for deadline planning and spotting days with high workload.

  • Tasks appear on the cell matching their due date, color-coded by priority (red=High, amber=Medium, blue=Low).
  • Completed tasks are shown with a strikethrough title in a muted color.
  • When a day has more than 3 tasks, the cell shows the first 3 and an overflow indicator, e.g. "+4 more". Click it to see all tasks for that date.
  • Navigate months using the left and right arrow buttons. Click Today to jump to the current month.
  • Click any task chip to open the Task Details drawer.

Timeline View

/pms/projects/:id/timeline — The Timeline (Gantt) view shows task bars along a horizontal time axis. It is the best view for managing deadline-heavy projects and understanding how tasks overlap.

  • Zoom levels — toggle between Weeks and Months using the zoom buttons in the top-right. Weeks is ideal for current sprint work; Months gives a higher-level roadmap perspective.
  • Navigation — use the left/right arrow buttons to scroll the timeline, or click Today to snap back to the current date.
  • Dragging bars — click and drag the left or right edge of a task bar to change its start or end date. Drag the middle of the bar to shift the whole date range without changing the duration.
  • Tasks without start or end dates are not visible on the timeline. Set due dates from the Task Details drawer to make them appear.
✓
Tip Use Timeline view when presenting a project roadmap to stakeholders. The Months zoom level gives a clean bird's-eye view of the entire quarter.

Project Overview

/pms/projects/:id/overview — A read-only analytical summary of the project's health. Useful for project owners and stakeholders who want progress information without navigating task lists.

  • Stats cards — Total Tasks, Completed, In Progress, Overdue.
  • Circular completion gauge — percentage of tasks completed, rendered as a donut chart.
  • Progress by Section — a horizontal progress bar per section showing how many tasks are done vs. total.
  • Priority distribution — breakdown of open tasks by priority (High / Medium / Low / None).
  • Activity timeline — recent project events (task created, completed, commented, etc.).
  • Top contributors — team members ranked by number of tasks completed this month.

Task Details Drawer

Clicking any task — from Board, List, Calendar, Timeline, My Tasks, or search results — opens a 480px drawer from the right side of the screen. The drawer overlays the current page with a semi-transparent scrim. Press Esc or click the scrim to close it.

Editing

  • Title — click the title text to enter inline-edit mode. Press Enter to save or Esc to cancel.
  • Complete toggle — the circle checkbox at the top-left. Click to mark the task done (or reopen it).

File Attachments

Click the Attach File button or drag files into the attachments zone. Uploaded files appear as a list with filename, size, and a download link. Editors and above can delete attachments using the trash icon next to each file.

Subtasks

In the Subtasks section, click + Add Subtask to create a child task. Subtasks have their own title, assignee, and due date. They are displayed as a checklist and count toward the parent task's completion indicator. Click a subtask title to open its own drawer.

Activity Log

A chronological feed of all changes made to the task — who changed what, and when. Entries include: task created, field updated (shows old → new value), comment added, attachment uploaded, assignee changed, and status changed.

Comments

Any project member with Commenter role or above can post comments. Comments support plain text. Click Post or press Ctrl+Enter to submit. Mentions (@username) trigger a notification to the mentioned user.

Goals

/pms/goals — Goals help you track high-level objectives that may span multiple projects. They appear as cards in a grid layout.

Creating a Goal

Click + New Goal. Enter a title and optionally a description and target date. Goals start in On Track status by default.

Goal Statuses

  • Achieved — goal has been met.
  • On Track — progress is proceeding normally.
  • At Risk — progress is slower than expected.
  • Off Track — significantly behind target.
  • Dropped — goal has been deprioritized or cancelled.

Filter the goals grid by any status using the filter buttons at the top of the page.

Goal Detail Drawer

Click any goal card to open the detail drawer. You can:

  • Update progress using manual tracking — enter a percentage (0–100%) directly.
  • Use auto tracking — link specific projects or tasks to the goal. Progress is then calculated automatically based on the completion rate of linked items.
  • Change the status via the status dropdown.
  • Add notes and update description.

Project Settings Owner

Access Project Settings from the project sidebar link or the Settings tab in any project view. This section is only accessible to project Owners (and workspace Admins).

General

  • Project name — inline edit.
  • Color — select from 12 color swatches. Changes the dot in the sidebar and the card accent border.
  • Description — multiline text field.

Archiving a Project

Archived projects are hidden from the sidebar and all lists but retain all data. Click Archive Project at the bottom of Settings. To restore, go to the workspace settings and find the Archived Projects list.

WMS 4. WMS Dashboard

The WMS Dashboard at /wms/dashboard provides an overview of your warehouse operations. Four KPI cards show:

  • Total Products — number of unique product SKUs defined in the workspace.
  • Warehouses — number of active warehouse locations.
  • Inventory Items — total line items across all warehouses (one product can have multiple inventory records in different warehouses).
  • Suppliers — number of registered suppliers.

The Top Inventory by Quantity bar chart shows the 10 highest-quantity inventory items, helping you quickly identify your largest stock positions.

Products

/wms/products — The Products page maintains the master catalog of all goods you track. Each product represents a unique SKU definition; actual stock quantities live in Inventory.

Products Table

A searchable, paginated table with columns: SKU (monospace), Name, Description, Unit Price, and Actions (edit/delete). Use the search bar above the table to filter by SKU or name — search is server-side ILIKE for fast results on large catalogs.

Creating and Editing Products

Click + Add Product (top right) to open the creation dialog. Fields:

  • SKU — unique stock-keeping unit identifier (e.g. "WIDGET-001").
  • Name — human-readable product name.
  • Description — optional details about the product.
  • Unit Price — base price per unit (stored in your workspace currency).

Click the pencil icon in the Actions column to edit an existing product. Click the trash icon to delete — deletion is permanent and will also remove associated inventory records.

Warehouses

/wms/warehouses — Physical or virtual storage locations where inventory is held. You must create at least one warehouse before adding inventory items.

The Warehouses table shows: Name, Location (address or description), and Status (Active / Inactive). Use the CRUD action buttons to create, edit, or delete warehouses. Deactivating a warehouse hides it from inventory filters but retains all associated inventory records.

Inventory

/wms/inventory — The Inventory page tracks actual stock quantities per warehouse. This is where day-to-day stock management happens.

Filtering by Warehouse

Use the Warehouse dropdown at the top of the page to view inventory for a specific location, or select "All Warehouses" to see everything combined.

Devices

/wms/devices — Track physical devices associated with warehouse operations, such as barcode scanners, RFID readers, forklifts, or any other equipment requiring lifecycle management.

The Devices list is paginated and shows: Device ID, Type, Location (which area it operates in), and Status. Click + Add Device to register a new device. Edit or delete existing devices using the action buttons in each row.

Suppliers

/wms/suppliers — A searchable directory of all suppliers you work with. Use this as the reference when creating purchase orders or procurement requests.

The Suppliers table shows: Name, Email, Phone, and Address. Search by supplier name. Full CRUD is available — create, view, edit, and delete supplier records. Deleting a supplier does not affect historical purchase records.

HRM 5. HRM Dashboard

The HRM Dashboard at /hrm/dashboard gives a real-time view of your workforce. KPI cards display:

  • Total Employees — headcount across all departments.
  • Departments — number of active departments.
  • Pending Leave Requests — leave requests awaiting approval.
  • Payroll Records — total payroll entries recorded this month.

The Employees by Department bar chart shows headcount distribution across departments — useful for spotting imbalances in organizational structure.

Employees

/hrm/employees — The central employee registry. The table lists all employees with columns: Name, Email, Position, Department, Hire Date, and Actions.

Use the search bar to filter by name or email (server-side ILIKE). Filter by department using the department dropdown. Paginated results load 20 records per page.

Employee Detail Page

Click any employee row to navigate to their detail page. The page is organized into four tabs:

Info Tab

Personal and employment information: full name, email, position title, hire date, and department. Admins and Members can edit these fields inline. Admins can delete the employee (this cascades and deletes all associated leave requests and payroll records).

Contracts Tab

Employment contract records showing contract type (permanent, fixed-term, probationary), start and end dates, and salary details. Multiple contracts can be stored to represent contract renewals or type changes over time.

Salary History Tab

A chronological list of all salary changes, showing effective date, previous salary, new salary, and reason for change. This provides a complete compensation history for the employee.

Leave Tab

Displays the employee's leave balance broken down by leave type (e.g. Annual Leave: 10 days remaining, Sick Leave: 5 days remaining), plus a list of all past and pending leave requests with their status badges.

Departments

/hrm/departments — Departments group employees into organizational units. The departments table shows Name, Description, and Headcount.

Full CRUD is available. Creating a department requires only a name; description is optional. Employees are assigned to departments via their profile. Deleting a department sets the department_id of all associated employees to null — employees are retained but become unassigned.

Org Chart

Click Org Chart (top right on the Departments page) to switch to a tree visualization. The org chart shows the department hierarchy with headcount displayed below each department node. Use this view for organizational planning and to share structure with new team members.

Positions

/hrm/positions — A catalog of job positions/titles used across the organization. Positions are separate from departments — a position like "Senior Engineer" can exist across multiple departments.

The Positions table shows Position Title, Department (optional association), and Description. CRUD operations are available. When creating employees, position titles from this catalog are suggested to ensure consistency in job title naming.

Leave Management

/hrm/leave — Leave management has two parts: defining leave types, and managing requests.

Leave Types

Leave types define the categories available to employees (e.g. Annual Leave, Sick Leave, Maternity Leave, Unpaid Leave). Each type has a Name and optional Description. Admin can create, edit, and delete leave types. Deleting a leave type does not affect existing requests that reference it.

Leave Requests

The leave requests table shows all requests across the workspace with columns: Employee, Leave Type, Start Date, End Date, Duration, and Status.

Creating a Request

Click + New Request. Select the employee, leave type, start date, and end date. End date must be on or after the start date. Click Submit — the request is created with status Pending.

Approval Workflow

Admin Pending requests show Approve and Reject action buttons. Approved requests update status to Approved; rejected requests update to Rejected. Employees are notified of the decision via the notification system.

Attendance

/hrm/attendance — Attendance tracking records when employees are present, late, or absent. The page includes:

  • Summary cards — today's totals for Present, Late, Absent, and On Leave.
  • Attendance log table — searchable list of attendance entries with employee name, date, check-in time, check-out time, and status.
  • Log entry creation — click + Log Attendance to manually record an entry for an employee. Select employee, date, check-in/out times, and status.

Payroll

/hrm/payroll — Payroll records store monthly compensation data for each employee. A-ERP stores payroll data but does not auto-calculate payroll — records are entered manually or via integration.

The payroll records table shows: Employee, Month (YYYY-MM format), Base Salary, Deductions, Bonus, and calculated Net Pay (Salary - Deductions + Bonus). Filter by employee using the search box.

Creating a Payroll Record

Click + Add Record. Select the employee and enter the month (e.g. 2026-03). Fill in Base Salary, Deductions, and Bonus (all default to 0.0 if left empty). Save to create the record. Multiple records for the same employee and month are allowed to represent corrections.

Insurance

/hrm/insurance — Insurance records track employee benefit coverage, including health insurance, social insurance, and other schemes. Each record is linked to an employee and includes the insurance type, provider, policy number, coverage period start/end dates, and premium amount.

Full CRUD operations are available. Use the employee filter to view insurance records for a specific team member.

Recruitment

/hrm/recruitment — The Recruitment module manages the full hiring pipeline from job opening to offer acceptance.

Job Openings

The main Recruitment page shows a table of all open and closed job positions. Each row shows the position title, department, number of candidates, and status (Open / Closed). Click + New Opening to create a job posting with title, department, description, and requirements.

Candidate Pipeline

Click any job opening row to navigate to its detail page. Candidates are tracked through these stages:

StageDescription
ScreeningInitial review of application and resume.
InterviewingActive interview process underway.
OfferedAn offer has been extended to the candidate.
HiredOffer accepted; candidate becomes an employee.
RejectedCandidate did not advance or declined the offer.

Managing Candidates

  • Add Candidate — enter name, email, phone, and resume link. Candidate starts at Screening.
  • Schedule Interview — click the calendar icon next to a candidate to schedule an interview. Enter date, time, format (in-person/video), and interviewer.
  • Record Feedback — after each interview, interviewers can add structured feedback with a score (1–5) and written notes. All feedback is visible on the candidate's detail card.
  • Make an Offer — when ready to hire, click Make Offer on the candidate card. Enter offer salary, start date, and any notes. The offer status updates to Sent. Record candidate response as Accepted or Rejected.
  • When an offer is Accepted, click Convert to Employee to automatically create an employee record from the candidate's data.

Onboarding

/hrm/onboarding — Onboarding checklists ensure new employees complete all necessary steps in their first days and weeks. Each employee has their own onboarding checklist instance.

Generating Default Checklists

Click Generate Defaults when viewing a new employee's onboarding to populate the checklist with your organization's standard tasks. Default categories typically include:

  • Documentation — ID verification, contract signing, tax forms.
  • IT Setup — laptop provisioning, account creation, tool access.
  • Orientation — company introduction, team meetings, policy review.
  • Training — required training modules to complete in the first 30 days.

Tracking Progress

Check off items as they are completed by clicking the checkbox next to each task. Progress is displayed as a percentage bar at the top of the employee's onboarding view. HR Admins can check off items on behalf of any employee.

KPIs & Performance

/hrm/performance — The Performance module uses KPI templates to define measurable metrics, then assigns those metrics to employees for specific periods.

KPI Templates

Templates define reusable metrics that can be assigned to multiple employees. Each template has:

  • Name — e.g. "Customer Satisfaction Score", "Code Review Rate".
  • Category — grouping such as "Sales", "Engineering", "Customer Support".
  • Unit — the measurement unit, e.g. %, number, USD.

KPI Assignments

Assign a KPI template to an employee for a specific period. Each assignment record stores:

  • Employee — who is being measured.
  • KPI Template — which metric applies.
  • Period — date range (e.g. Q1 2026, January 2026).
  • Target — the goal value (e.g. 90 for "90% Customer Satisfaction").
  • Progress % — current achievement, updated manually or via integration. Displayed as a colored progress bar (green when at/above target, amber when close, red when far below).

Performance Reviews

/hrm/reviews — Performance Reviews enable structured evaluation cycles. Admins create review cycles (e.g. "Annual Review 2026") that define the review period and participants. Employees and managers complete feedback forms for each review cycle.

The Reviews page lists all active and past review cycles. Click a cycle to view all associated feedback forms. Each form captures: reviewer name, reviewee name, scores on competency dimensions, and written feedback. Completed reviews are locked for editing to preserve the record.

Training

/hrm/training — Manage training programs and track employee enrollment and completion. The Training page has two tabs:

Programs Tab

Lists all training programs with: Name, Trainer (person or organization delivering the training), Start Date, End Date, and Budget. Click + New Program to create a training program. Edit or delete programs from the action buttons.

Enrollments Tab

Shows which employees are enrolled in which programs. Columns: Employee, Program, Status (Enrolled / Completed / Dropped), and Score (optional assessment score on completion). Click + Enroll Employee to add an enrollment. Update status and score as the training progresses.

Offboarding

/hrm/offboarding — The Offboarding module manages the employee exit process. When an employee submits a resignation or is terminated, an offboarding record is created.

Resignation Records

The main Offboarding page shows a table of all offboarding cases with: Employee, Last Working Day, Reason, and Status. Click + New Offboarding to initiate the process for an employee.

Offboarding Detail Page

Click an offboarding record to open the detail page, which has two sections:

  • Handover Tasks — a checklist of items the departing employee must complete before their last day, such as "Hand over project documentation", "Remove personal data", "Return laptop". Add custom tasks or use default templates. Check off items as they are completed.
  • Exit Interview — structured feedback form capturing the employee's reasons for leaving, satisfaction scores, and suggestions for improvement. Results are stored privately and only visible to workspace Admins.

Assets

/hrm/assets — The Assets module tracks company equipment assigned to employees, such as laptops, phones, access cards, and furniture.

Asset Catalog

Assets have a lifecycle tracked via status:

  • Available — in stock, ready to assign.
  • Assigned — currently with an employee.
  • Maintenance — undergoing repair or service.
  • Retired — decommissioned, no longer in use.

Assigning Assets

Select an asset with Available status and click Assign to Employee. Choose the employee and optional assignment date/notes. The asset status changes to Assigned and appears on the employee's profile. When an employee leaves, use the Return action to set the asset back to Available (or Maintenance if it needs servicing).

Contacts

/crm/contacts — The Contacts page is your complete directory of people and companies you interact with. The default landing page for the CRM module.

Contacts Table

A searchable, paginated table with columns: Name, Email, Phone, Company, Created, and Actions. The search box filters across name, email, and company simultaneously (server-side ILIKE). Results are paginated 20 per page.

Creating a Contact

Click + Add Contact. Fill in:

  • Name — required. Full name of the person or company contact (up to 255 characters).
  • Email — optional. Must be a valid email format.
  • Phone — optional. Up to 50 characters.
  • Company — optional. Organization the contact belongs to.

Click the pencil icon in the Actions column to edit any field. Admin can delete contacts — this does not delete associated deals, but the contact reference on those deals is cleared.

Deals

/crm/deals — Deals represent sales opportunities. Each deal is linked to a contact (optional) and progresses through the pipeline stages from Lead to closed.

Deals Table

Columns: Title, Value (in USD), Stage (colored badge), Contact, Created, and Actions. Filter deals by stage using the stage filter buttons above the table. Multiple stages can be selected simultaneously.

Creating a Deal

Click + Add Deal. Fill in:

  • Title — descriptive name for the deal (e.g. "Acme Corp — Enterprise License Q2").
  • Value — estimated deal value in USD (defaults to 0.0).
  • Stage — starting stage; defaults to Lead.
  • Contact — optional link to an existing contact.

Moving a Deal Through Stages

Click the pencil icon on a deal to edit it. Change the Stage dropdown to advance or regress the deal. For example, move from Qualified to Proposal once a proposal has been sent. When a deal is won, set stage to Closed Won; if lost, set to Closed Lost. Closed deals are excluded from the pipeline value calculation but remain in the table for reporting.

✓
Pipeline Health A healthy pipeline has deals distributed across early stages (Lead/Qualified) with a narrowing funnel toward Closed Won. If most deals are stuck at Proposal or Negotiation, review your follow-up cadence.

  • Click a notification — navigates to the relevant record (e.g. the task that was mentioned in or commented on).
  • Mark all read — button at the top of the dropdown. Clears the unread count and marks all notifications as read.

How Notifications Work

A-ERP delivers notifications via two complementary methods:

  • Server-Sent Events (SSE) — a persistent connection from your browser to the server receives notifications in real time while you are actively using the app. New notifications appear instantly without any page refresh.
  • Polling — every 30 seconds, the app polls for new notifications. This acts as a fallback if the SSE connection drops, ensuring you never miss a notification.

You receive notifications for: task assignments, task mentions, comments on tasks you follow, due date reminders, and leave request approval/rejection decisions.

8. Tips & Best Practices

Project Management

✓
Use Goals to connect strategy to execution. Link your Q1 projects directly to a company Goal. When project tasks get completed, the Goal progress updates automatically — giving leadership real-time visibility into strategic objectives.
✓
Custom fields add project-specific structure. Don't over-rely on the description field for structured data. If your engineering team needs to track "Story Points" or "PR Link" per task, add custom fields to that project. This keeps task data queryable and consistent.
✓
Tags work across projects; sections don't. Use tags (e.g. "bug", "design", "blocked") for cross-project categorization. Use sections/columns for within-project status stages. A task can have multiple tags but belongs to exactly one section.
✓
Use Timeline view for deadline-heavy projects. Switch to Timeline before sprint planning to visualize date overlaps. Drag task bars to resolve scheduling conflicts before they become problems.

My Tasks Routine

✓
Start every day on My Tasks. The Overdue and Today buckets tell you exactly what must be addressed first. Review the Upcoming bucket to plan your week. This habit prevents tasks from falling through the cracks across multiple projects.

Warehouse

✓
Set min thresholds for every inventory item. A minimum threshold of 0 means the Low Stock badge only appears when you're completely out. Set realistic reorder points (typically a 1–2 week supply buffer) so you can restock before running out.

Human Resources

✓
Generate onboarding defaults immediately after hiring. When you convert a recruited candidate to an employee, go directly to their Onboarding page and click Generate Defaults. This ensures no step is missed during the first day chaos.
✓
Use KPI templates for consistency. Define your standard performance metrics as templates once (e.g. "Customer Satisfaction Score %", "Tickets Resolved Per Week") and reuse them across quarterly assignments. This makes performance reviews comparable across periods.

CRM

✓
Monitor deals stuck in the same stage. Filter the Deals table by stage and sort by Created date. Deals that have been at the same stage for longer than your average sales cycle length need attention — either advance them or mark as Closed Lost to keep your pipeline accurate.
✓
Keep your pipeline value realistic. Include only genuinely qualified opportunities. Inflated pipeline value (deals unlikely to close) makes the CRM Dashboard misleading. Move speculative leads to Closed Lost early so forecasting stays accurate.

Security

⚠
Never share your account credentials. Each team member should have their own account. Use workspace roles to give colleagues the access they need. If someone needs temporary access, assign them as Guest rather than sharing a login.
